Employability

There are two types of recording related to employability. One is the employability review, the other is the employability evaluation.

The employability review ascertains the person's current position, if they require employability support, their goals and the conclusion to the support.

The employability evaluation asks five questions, each rated on a scale of 1-6 with one being the worst case scenario and six being the best. These questions are:

  • Do you feel you could take a job? (I'm not ready to work yet --- I could take a job)
  • How confident do you feel? (I really lack confidence --- I feel very confident)
  • How much support do you feel you need each day? (I need a lot of daily support --- I need very little daily support)
  • Do you feel good about yourself? (I don't feel good about myself --- I feel really good about myself)
  • Do you have additional income to state benefits? (My only income is from benefits --- I need benefits less)
